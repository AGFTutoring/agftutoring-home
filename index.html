Why does water have a higher boiling point than H₂S?
A classic exam question — and the answer is hydrogen bonding. Water forms up to 4 hydrogen bonds per molecule because the O–H bond is highly polar. H₂S cannot — sulfur's lower electronegativity means the S–H bond isn't polar enough. The result: much stronger intermolecular forces in water, and a significantly higher boiling point.

About AGF

Thirty years of
diagnostic teaching

Alastair Fisher studied Chemistry at the University of Edinburgh and Linguistics at the University of Surrey. He founded a tutorial college in 1999 and has been teaching and mentoring students ever since — building up a body of knowledge and a diagnostic method that is now the foundation of everything AGF does.

AGF Tutoring was founded on a simple conviction: that students deserve teaching which does not merely cover content, but uncovers the precise reason misunderstanding has taken root. For over three decades, Alastair has put that conviction into practice through diagnostic tutoring, mentoring, and admissions guidance.

The diagnostic method is simple in principle: find the gap first. Not the syllabus point the student is already comfortable with, but the precise concept where understanding breaks down. Build from there. Test. Repeat. The result — consistently — is students who understand rather than remember, and who perform under exam conditions rather than freezing.

Before teaching anything new, the gap is identified: which hidden misconception, weak definition, missing step, or careless habit is generating errors downstream. This is not guesswork. It requires deep subject knowledge and the pattern recognition that comes only from thousands of hours of teaching.

The access problem is real. High-quality tutoring is expensive, limited by time, and unavailable at the moment when many students most need help. The AGF Study Companion is built to extend AGF's diagnostic approach beyond the live session — available at any hour, at a fraction of the cost of one-to-one tuition.

About

An unusual combination
of disciplines

My academic background began in chemistry at the University of Edinburgh, where my studies moved deeply into chemical physics — a discipline requiring a rigorous command of mathematics and a strong grounding in the positive sciences. Some years later, I completed a Master's degree in linguistics at the University of Surrey, focusing on corpus linguistics.

What emerged from that combination was something relatively rare: a tutor equally at home in the hard sciences and in the precise analytical demands of language-based examinations.

Scientific depth
Advanced preparation across mathematics, physics, and chemistry, grounded in 60,000+ hours of one-to-one teaching at the highest levels.
Linguistic precision
A formal understanding of language and cognition informing preparation for LNAT, SAT, ACT, GRE, GMAT, and IELTS.

Over thirty years I have worked with more than five thousand students across different countries and academic systems. Through that experience I have developed a way of thinking about how students learn — how misunderstandings arise, and how complex ideas can be made clear without being oversimplified.

AGF Tutoring is a premier, boutique online service providing top-tier private tutoring and mentorship to help students gain admission and scholarships to prestigious universities globally — at undergraduate, postgraduate, and PhD level.

The AGF Method

Teaching as a diagnostic process

Every session begins not with a syllabus, but with a question: what does this student currently believe to be true? The answer to that question determines everything that follows.

01
Diagnose
Identify the precise point at which understanding breaks down — not the symptom, but the underlying misunderstanding, which often sits several steps earlier than it appears.
02
Rebuild
Reconstruct the concept from first principles, beginning with intuition rather than formalism. What is actually happening here? What does this idea represent?
03
Clarify
Introduce precise language and formal structure only once the intuition is secure. Understanding precedes technique — never the reverse.
04
Test
Confirm understanding through carefully chosen diagnostic questions. A student who can explain a concept clearly has genuinely understood it.
05
Extend
Move from competence to mastery — from being able to solve a problem to understanding why the solution works, and how to approach unfamiliar variations.

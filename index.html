Teaching philosophy

My approach is diagnostic first. Before teaching anything new, I identify precisely where a student’s understanding breaks down: which hidden misconception, weak definition, missing step, or careless habit is generating errors downstream. This is not guesswork. It requires deep subject knowledge and the pattern recognition that comes only from thousands of hours of teaching.

Every student receives a bespoke programme of study. There is no standard curriculum, no one-size-fits-all approach. The plan changes as the student changes — I diagnose continuously, not just at the start.

Why I built the AI Companion

Bloom’s 1984 research remains one of the most powerful arguments ever made for personal tutoring: students improve most when feedback is immediate, individual, and precisely targeted. The problem is access. High-quality tutoring is expensive, limited by time, and unavailable at the moment when many students most need help. I built the AGF Study Companion to extend AGF’s diagnostic approach beyond the live session — available at any hour, and at a fraction of the cost of one-to-one tuition.

Chemistry · WCH11
Why does water have a higher boiling point than H₂S?
Great question — and a classic exam favourite. The key is hydrogen bonding. Water's O–H bonds are highly polar, so each molecule can form up to four hydrogen bonds with neighbours. H₂S only forms weak London dispersion forces — sulfur's lower electronegativity means S–H bonds aren't polar enough for H-bonding. More energy is needed to overcome water's network of H-bonds, hence the much higher boiling point (+100°C vs −60°C).

Questions students and parents ask

Before you
try the companion

How is this different from a general chatbot?

The companion is organised around named qualifications, exam boards and curated AGF subject notes. Its purpose is teaching and exam practice—not general conversation.

Will it simply give students the answer?

AGF is designed to explain the reasoning, identify likely misconceptions and test understanding again. Students should still show their own working and follow their school’s rules on AI use.

What can I try without paying?

You can explore Ask mode and complete three free quizzes without creating an account. The current limits and subscription price are shown before payment.

Can the companion replace a teacher or tutor?

No. It provides immediate practice and explanation between lessons. For deeper diagnosis, accountability or complex individual needs, students can work directly with Alastair.

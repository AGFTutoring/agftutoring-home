Chemistry Unit 1 · WCH11
Why does water have a higher boiling point than H₂S?
Great question — and a classic exam favourite. The key is hydrogen bonding. Each water molecule can form up to 4 hydrogen bonds because the O–H bond is highly polar. H₂S forms only weak London forces — sulfur's lower electronegativity means the S–H bond isn't polar enough for H-bonding.

About

Alastair Fisher

My work over thirty years has centred on one question: how students come to understand complex ideas clearly, and how that understanding translates into performance under examination conditions.

My academic background began in chemistry at the University of Edinburgh, with emphasis on chemical physics and mathematics. A later Master's degree in linguistics at the University of Surrey gave me a formal understanding of how meaning is constructed and communicated — a combination that allows me to work across the sciences, mathematics, and reasoning-intensive examinations such as the SAT, GRE, GMAT, and LNAT.

Over 60,000 hours of teaching, a consistent pattern has become clear: most academic difficulty arises not from a lack of ability, but from a lack of clarity. The AGF Study Companion is an attempt to make that accumulated diagnostic experience accessible to every student, not as a replacement for individual teaching, but as an extension of it.
